To make it easier for you to find the information you need, we’ve compiled a list of answers to some of the most common questions we receive. What is the NDIS? The NDIS (National Disability Insurance Scheme) is an Australian government initiative designed to support individuals with permanent and significant disabilities. It provides funding to help with services and supports that improve quality of life, independence, and participation in the community. Who is eligible for NDIS funding? To be eligible for NDIS funding, individuals must be under 65 years of age, an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and have a permanent and significant disability that impacts their daily life. Eligibility is assessed through an NDIS access request. What services do you offer under the NDIS? We provide a range of NDIS services including:Personal care and supportTherapeutic supports (e.g., physiotherapy, speech therapy)Household tasks assistanceSupported employmentTransport assistanceCommunity access and social participationPlan management services How do I apply for NDIS funding? To apply for NDIS funding, you will need to make an access request through the NDIS. This involves providing medical evidence and personal details to confirm your eligibility. We can assist you with the application process if needed. Can I choose my service provider? Yes, as an NDIS participant, you have the freedom to choose the service providers that best meet your needs. You can either self-manage your NDIS plan, use a plan manager, or let the NDIS manage your plan.What is the difference between plan management and self-management?Self-management means you manage your NDIS funds directly, including paying providers and keeping track of your budget.Plan management means a registered plan manager will handle the financial and administrative tasks related to your NDIS plan, while you maintain control over your supports and services. How do I know what services are covered by the NDIS? The NDIS funds supports that are considered reasonable and necessary to help you achieve your goals. These can include healthcare, community access, assistive technology, and other disability-related supports. You can discuss your goals and needs with your support coordinator to ensure your plan covers appropriate services. How do I make a claim or access my NDIS funding? Claims can be made via the NDIS portal or through your plan manager, depending on how your plan is managed. You will need to submit invoices for the services you have received, and these will be processed accordingly. What if I need support outside of regular office hours? Some of our services may be available outside of regular business hours. Please contact us directly to discuss your needs, and we will try to accommodate you where possible. Can I change my NDIS plan or services? Yes, NDIS plans are reviewed regularly, and you can request a review if your circumstances or needs change. You can contact your NDIS planner or support coordinator to discuss any changes to your plan. How do I contact your business for support or to book a service? You can contact us via phone, email, or our online booking system. Our contact details are available on our website, and we are happy to assist you with any questions or service bookings. What if I have a complaint or feedback about my services? We value your feedback and are committed to ensuring a positive experience. If you have a complaint or suggestion, please reach out to our customer service team directly. We have a formal complaints process in place to address any concerns. Yes, all of our staff are highly trained and experienced in providing support to NDIS participants. We ensure that our team undergoes regular training to stay updated on the latest NDIS policies and practices. How can I give you feedback on my experience?
